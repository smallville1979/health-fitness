🥦 Eat Healthy Cheap

Healthy Eating
on a Tight Budget

Eating healthy is not expensive if you know what to buy. It's only expensive if you're buying the wrong things or wasting food.

🥚

The Cheap Protein Staples

Eggs (6 for £1), tinned tuna (40p), tinned sardines (50p), chicken thighs (not breasts — half the price), lentils, chickpeas, kidney beans. These are all complete or near-complete protein sources. A family can eat well on £30/week buying these.

✅ High protein, very low cost
🛒

Aldi & Lidl — Just Use Them

Aldi and Lidl are genuinely 30–50% cheaper than Tesco and Sainsbury's on most items. The quality is the same or better on fresh produce. If you shop at a big supermarket out of habit, change it. You'll notice it immediately in your bank account.

💡 Same food, 30–50% cheaper
🍌

Yellow Sticker / Reduced Section

Every supermarket reduces meat, bread, veg and ready meals near closing time. Usually 5–8pm for most stores. These items are at 10–30% of full price. Buy and freeze meat immediately. Meal planning around yellow sticker shopping can cut your food bill massively.

💡 Check the reduced section daily — freeze meat
🍲

Batch Cook — Save Time and Money

Cook a big pot of soup, stew, chilli or curry once and eat it 4-5 days. Freeze half. A 10-litre pot of lentil soup costs £3 and feeds you for a week. Cooking from scratch is always cheaper than ready meals. Always.

✅ Cook once, eat all week

✂️ Cancel What You Don't Use

Check Your Bank.
Stop the Bleeding.

Most people have at least one subscription they forgot about. Some have five. Check your bank statement right now — it takes 5 minutes and could save you £50+ a month.

💬 Real example

"A friend got stung for months by a website she'd never heard of — wireclub.com type thing. Never used it. Unauthorised charge. The bank reversed it and she got compensation. If something looks wrong on your statement — dispute it. Banks have to investigate. Don't just leave it."

🔍

Check Your Bank Statement Monthly

Open your banking app. Go to direct debits and subscriptions. Write down every recurring charge. Ask yourself: "Did I use this last month?" If no — cancel it. If you can't remember what it is — look it up, then cancel it.

⚠️ Do this now — takes 5 minutes
✂️

Cancel Direct Debits You Don't Use

You can cancel any direct debit directly from your banking app — no need to contact the company first. Go to Payments → Direct Debits → Cancel. If the company charges you after you cancel, that's an unauthorised transaction and your bank must refund it.

✅ Cancel inside your banking app — instant
🚨

Dispute Unauthorised Charges

See a charge you didn't authorise? Call your bank immediately. Say "I want to raise a chargeback on this transaction — I did not authorise this payment." Banks are legally required to investigate. Most refund instantly. You can also ask for compensation for distress caused — some banks pay £25–50 without argument.

💡 Chargeback — your legal right
🎵
